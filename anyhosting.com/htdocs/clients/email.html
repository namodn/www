This document describes how to setup your email program with your AnyHosting mail account.

Incoming (POP or IMAP) and outgoing (SMTP) mail servers should both be set to mail.anyhosting.com.

Email software:
  • MS Outlook Express (5 and 6)
  • MS Outlook 97
  • MS Outlook 98 and 2000
  • MS Outlook XP/2002
  • Netscape 6/7 (Mozilla 1.x)
  • Eudora 5.x
MS Outlook Express (5 and 6)
  1. From the Outlook Express "Tools" menu, choose "Accounts".
  2. Click the index-tab labeled "Mail".
  3. Click the "Add" button, and choose "Mail".
  4. Type your name in the "Display Name" box. Click "Next".
  5. Type your email address in the "E-mail address" box. Click "Next".
  6. Under "My incoming mail server is a _____ server":
    Choose POP3.
    In the "Incoming mail server" field, type: mail.anyhosting.com
    In the "Outgoing mail server" field, type: mail.anyhosting.com
    Click "Next".
  7. Type your account name in the field provided. (This is the same as your AnyHosting login ID.)
    Type your password in the "Password" field.
    Click "Next".
  8. In the "Internet mail account name" field, type "AnyHosting Email", or whatever you like. (This is just the name that'll show up in your "Accounts" list; it won't appear on the mail you send.)
  9. Click "Finish".

    (Some versions of Outlook Express have another "Next" button at this point instead of a "Finish" button. If so, click "Next" and proceed to:)
  10. Select your connection type.
    (Hint: if you use a 56K modem attached to your computer, you connect using your phone line. If you get your Internet connection via DSL, cable-modem, or an office network, you're connecting through a LAN.)
    Click "Next".
  11. Click "Finish".
MS Outlook 97:
  1. From the Outlook "Tools" menu, choose "Services". (The Services window should pop up, with the "Services" _tab_ already in front.)
  2. Click "Add", choose "Internet Mail", and click "OK". (Or, if you already have an "Internet Mail" entry that you'd like to change, just highlight it and click "Properties".)
  3. The Properties window will open, with the "General" tab on top.
    Type your name in the "Full Name" field.
    Type your email address in the "E-Mail Address" field.
    In the "Internet Mail Server" field, type:
    mail.anyhosting.com
    In the "Account Name" field, type your POP account name. (This is the same as your AnyHosting login ID.)
    Then type your password in the "Password" field.
  4. Click the "Connection" index-tab.
    Select your connection type.
    (Hint: if you use a 56K modem attached to your computer, you "connect using the modem". If you get your Internet connection via DSL, cable-modem, or an office network, you're "using the network" to connect.)
  5. Click OK.
MS Outlook 98 and 2000:
  1. From the Outlook "Tools" menu, choose "Services". (The Services window should pop up, with the "Services" _tab_ already in front.)
  2. Click "Add", choose "Internet Mail" or "Internet E-mail", and click "OK". (Or, if you already have an "Internet Mail" entry that you'd like to change, just highlight it and click "Properties".)
  3. The Properties window will open, with the "General" tab on top.
    Under "Type the name by which you would like to refer to these servers...", type "AnyHosting Email" or something similar.
    Type your name in the "Name" field.
    Type your company/organization in the "Organization" field, if you like.
    Type your email address in the "E-mail Address" field.
    (You can leave the "Reply address" blank.)
  4. Select the "Servers" index tab.
    In "Incoming mail (POP3)", type: mail.anyhosting.com
    In "Outgoing mail (SMTP)", type: mail.anyhosting.com
    If you're given two options for log-on, choose "Log on using (Account name & Password)".
    In the "Account Name" field, type your POP account name. (This is the same as your AnyHosting login ID.)
    Then type your password in the "Password" field.
  5. Click the "Connection" index-tab.
    Select your connection type.
    (Hint: if you use a 56K modem attached to your computer, you "connect using my phone line". If you get your Internet connection via DSL, cable-modem, or an office network, you're "using my local area network (LAN)" to connect.)
  6. Click OK.
MS Outlook XP/2002:
  1. From the Outlook "Tools" menu, choose "E-mail Accounts".
  2. Choose "Add a new e-mail account". Click "Next".
  3. Choose "POP3". Click "Next".
  4. Under "User Information":
    • Type your name in the "Your Name" field.
    • Type your email address in the "E-mail Address" field.
    Under "Logon Information":
    • Type your email account username in the "User Name" field. (This is the same as your AnyHosting login ID.)
    • Type your password in the "Password" field.
    • Type your email account username in the "User Name" field. (This is the same as your AnyHosting login ID.)
      Type your password in the "Password" field.
    Under "Server Information":
    • In the "Incoming mail server" field, type: mail.anyhosting.com
    • In the "Outgoing mail server (SMTP)" field, type: mail.anyhosting.com
    Click "Next".
  5. Click "Finish".
Netscape 6/7 (Mozilla 1.x)
  1. Start from your "Mail & Newsgroups" window in Netscape or Mozilla. If you're starting in the Web browser window, just go to the "Window" menu and select "Mail & Newsgroups". (In Netscape 6 only, it's the "Tasks" menu.)
  2. From the Mail & Newsgroups window, go to the "Edit" menu and select "Mail & Newsgroups Account Settings".
    A new window will appear.
  3. Click the "Add Account..." button, on the lower left of this window.
    Make sure "Email account" is selected, and click "Next".
  4. Type your name and email address in the fields provided. Click "Next".
  5. Select "POP".
    In "Incoming Server", type: mail.anyhosting.com
    Click "Next".
  6. Type your account's username in the "User Name" field. (This is the same as your AnyHosting login ID.)
    Click "Next".
  7. Type a name that'll identify this profile to you. Click "Next".
  8. Look over your settings to make sure they're okay, and click "Finish".
  9. Now you'll be returned to the "Account Settings" window. Select the profile you just created, and click the "Set as Default" button to make this profile your default one.
Eudora 5.x
  1. From the Eudora "Tools" menu, choose "Options".
  2. Select "Getting Started" from the list on the left side.
    Under "Getting Started", on the right side:
    • Type your name in the "Real name" field.
    • Type your email address in the "Return address" field.
    • Under "Mail Server (Incoming)", type:
      mail.anyhosting.com
    • Under "Login Name", type your email username.
      (This is the same as your AnyHosting login ID.)
    • Under "SMTP Server (Outgoing)", type:
      mail.anyhosting.com
  3. Select "Incoming Mail" from the list on the left side.
    Under "Incoming Mail", on the right side:
    • Select "POP".
    • Select "Passwords" under "Authentication style".
    • Other options on this page are up to you.
  4. Click "OK".
Macintosh email software:
  • MS Outlook Express
  • Mac OS X Mail
  • Netscape 6/7 (Mozilla 1.x)
  • Eudora 5.x
MS Outlook Express
  1. From the Outlook Express "Tools" menu, choose "Accounts".
  2. Click the index-tab labeled "Mail".
  3. Click and hold the "New" button, and drag the pointer to "Mail".
  4. Type your name in the "Display Name" box. Click the right arrow.
  5. Type your email address in the "E-mail address" box.
    Click the right arrow.
  6. In this screen:
    • Under "My incoming mail server is a _____ server":
      Choose POP3.
    • In the "Incoming mail server" field, type:
      mail.anyhosting.com
    • In the "Outgoing mail server" field, type:
      mail.anyhosting.com
    • Click the right arrow.
  7. Select "Log on using: Account ID" and type your "Account ID" in the field provided.
    (This is the same as your AnyHosting login ID.)
    Then type your password in the "Password" field.
    Click the right arrow.
  8. In the "Account name" field, type "AnyHosting Email", or whatever you like. (This is just the name that'll show up in your "Accounts" list; it won't appear on the mail you send.)
  9. Click "Finish".
Mac OS X Mail
  1. From the Mail program's "Mail" menu, choose "Preferences".
    A new window will appear, entitled "Accounts".
    (If it doesn't say "Accounts", click the Accounts icon.)
  2. Click the "Add Account" button.
    • Under "Account Type", select "POP".
    • Under "Description", type "AnyHosting email", or something else that identifies this profile to you.
    • Type your email address and full name in the fields provided.
    • In "Incoming Mail Server", type:
      mail.anyhosting.com
    • In "User Name", type your mail account username.
      (This is the same as your AnyHosting login ID.)
    • Check "Outgoing Mail Server"; it should say "mail.anyhosting.com".
  3. Click "OK" to return to your list of accounts. Your new profile should be on the list now.
  4. Close the window.

    In "Incoming Server", type: mail.anyhosting.com
    Click the right arrow.
  6. Type your account's username in the "User Name" field.
    (This is the same as your AnyHosting login ID.)
    Click the right arrow.
  7. Type a name that'll identify this profile to you.
    Click the right arrow.
  8. Look over your settings to make sure they're okay, and click "Finish".

Eudora 5.x
  1. From the Eudora "Special" menu, choose "Settings".
  2. Select "Getting Started" from the list on the left side.
    Under "Getting Started", on the right side:
    • Under "Username", type your email username.
      (This is the same as your AnyHosting login ID.)
    • Under "Mail Server", type:
      mail.anyhosting.com
    • Type your name in the "Real name" field.
    • You can leave "SMTP Server" blank, or type:
      mail.anyhosting.com
    • Type your email address in the "Return address" field.
  3. Select "Checking Mail" from the list on the left side.
    Under "Checking Mail", on the right side:
    • Select "POP".
      If you selected "POP" above, make sure Authentication is set to "Passwords".
    • Other options on this page are up to you.
  4. Click "OK".
